Banking on the Future

Category: Future & Adaptation

A participatory process is first used to achieve consensus on desired management strategy, based on upside projections. The projected yield for the chosen strategy is then allocated, via a multi-criterion allocation formula based on fishery values.

Good Fit For:

Species with a well-known life history that are resilient to shifts

Essentials:

  • Information about the species to populate parameters for modeling
  • Willingness of community to collaborate/cooperate
  • Criteria for eligibility

Potential Trade-Offs:

  • Challenging for new entrants

Additional Notes:

What happens if projections are too low? Too high? You need defaults.

Flexible Portfolios

Category: Future & Adaptation

Allocate shares of a multi-species fishery or species complex. Fishermen receive a % of the whole group of species (e.g., % of the “groundfish fishery” or of a “flatfish complex”). Then, assess stock status of each species each year and distribute actual pounds of each that can be caught that season. Permanent % shares are stable while the annual pounds fluctuate with abundance.

Good Fit For:

Multispecies fisheries, fisheries where species undergo wide swings in abundance

Essentials:

  • Track abundance at species level
  • Track catch at species level
  • Administration capacity for changing quota pound amount annually
  • Ability and willingness of fishery participant to trade annual QP rapidly

Potential Trade-Offs:

  • May create challenge with fishery specialization/ niche, which can create challenges with economic viability
  • Requires a lot of data

Additional Notes:

Further stability could be created if quotas straddle a larger space and substantially cover the full species ranges.
